About the Project

I've always thought my hometown was pretty awesome!

Known as the City of Arts and Innovation, where corporate headquarters in skyscrapers sit beside historic homes from colonial America. I find it crazy that this was the place I grew up. I've walked the same paths as George Washington and I've sat in theater seats where audiences watched Harry Houdini perform. I think there are so many fascinating stories to be told about my hometown. This interactive website explores the historical narrative of the city with anecdotes of my own memories and perspectives. Through interactive elements and hyperlinks, different aspects of the city are woven together and presented in a casual and somewhat humorous manner.

When a place is your hometown, you view it as the setting of your everyday life, full of the mundane but also the extremely personal. As a visitor, you often consider the historical significance of a place or the special events that have occurred, rather than considering the everyday experience.

This project is my way of exploring the overlap between the stories that will be forever documented in the historical narrative of Winston-Salem as well as my own experiences and perspectives of the city as a hometown.

I invite you, as you explore my hometown, to consider the portrait of your own hometown...
